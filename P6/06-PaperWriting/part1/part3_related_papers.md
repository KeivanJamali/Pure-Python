# Related Papers for Part 3: Examining the Impact of Waste on the Environment

Here is a list of comprehensive academic review papers and studies related to the environmental impacts of construction waste (water pollution, soil pollution, natural resources consumption, and greenhouse gas emissions). 

| Paper Title | Link (URL or DOI) | Summary/Review |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Environmental impacts of construction and demolition waste management alternatives: A review | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.wasman.2011.08.014 | This comprehensive review evaluates Life Cycle Assessments (LCAs) of various construction waste management scenarios. It highlights that integrating recycling processes significantly mitigates greenhouse gas emissions and curtails the rapid consumption of virgin natural resources. |
| Life cycle assessment of construction and demolition waste management: A review | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jclepro.2019.03.186 | The study investigates the holistic environmental consequences of construction and demolition (C&D) waste using LCA methodologies. It finds measurable reductions in water and soil pollution when waste is actively diverted from landfills to specialized recycling facilities. |
| Evaluating the environmental impacts of construction and demolition waste management using life cycle assessment | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.resconrec.2017.01.024 | This paper models the environmental footprint of different waste disposal methods within the construction industry. The authors emphasize that uncontrolled landfilling of construction waste leads to severe heavy metal leaching, presenting a dire risk to surrounding soil and groundwater quality. |
| A review on the management of construction and demolition waste and its environmental impacts | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jclepro.2016.03.023 | This research provides an in-depth analysis of how unauthorized dumping of construction debris impacts local ecosystems. It details how chemical runoffs from C&D waste severely contaminate surface water resources and increase localized carbon footprints. |
| Carbon footprint of recycling construction and demolition waste | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.enbuild.2015.02.042 | Focused primarily on atmospheric impacts, this study quantifies the greenhouse gas emissions associated with transporting, processing, and landfilling construction waste. It demonstrates that local recycling infrastructures drastically reduce transport-related emissions and energy consumption. |
| Environmental and economic assessment of construction and demolition waste management | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.wasman.2012.06.012 | By examining both the ecological and financial systems of waste management, this paper shows how sustainable practices reduce raw natural resource consumption. It highlights strategies that prevent long-term soil toxicity and reduce the heavy water usage associated with virgin material extraction. |
| Quantifying the environmental impacts of construction and demolition waste management scenarios | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jclepro.2016.01.070 | This paper uses impact categories such as eutrophication and eco-toxicity to measure C&D waste damage. The authors reveal that proper grading and sorting of demolition waste prevents toxic substances from leaching into aquifers and agricultural soil over time. |
| The impact of construction waste on natural resources and the environment | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.buildenv.2010.02.013 | This study examines the life cycle of building materials to show the direct correlation between construction waste and the depletion of natural aggregates. It provides empirical evidence on how excessive material waste inflates both indirect greenhouse gas emissions and watershed degradation. |
| Life cycle assessment of recycling of construction and demolition waste | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.buildenv.2008.03.003 | This highly cited paper conducts a deep LCA on recycled versus virgin building materials. It conclusively proves that recycling concrete and steel from demolished structures saves vast amounts of natural resources and dramatically cuts energy-related greenhouse gas emissions. |
| Construction and demolition waste management and its environmental impacts: A brief review | https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scs.2018.06.024 | Exploring urbanization trends, this review details the heavy environmental toll of construction waste on city borders. It underscores how localized dumping leads to soil degradation, while the decomposition of certain building materials actively releases volatile organic compounds and greenhouse gases. |
